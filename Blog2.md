# Blog 2: Game design Document
In this document the basic game design philosophy behind the project for the GMD1 course will be outlined. This includes the game description, lore background, game mechanics and the gameplay itself.

# Sword clash world
The name of the game is Sword clash. We are within a medival world and happen to chance upon a tournament organised by a kingdom with the goal of finding the strongest person to marry the princes or eat all the food on the victory buffet table (depends who you ask). 

# Gameplay
The game is an arena, first person, hack and slash action game inspired by chivalry, as such the gameplay shares the similarities of simulating a battle between knights where the goal of the player is to beat the opponent by attacking him from various directions, and avoid losing by defending themselves from the same. It is a tournament style series of 1v1 battles with the end goal to win them all. The tone of the game is supposed to be more humorous and the focus of the gameplay is not on the tehnical difficulty of the fights(feints, kicks, fast change attack directions) but rather on the sandbox arena, and the humourus nature of the game(think funny sounds, character lines and emotes). Furthermore there is an additional multiplayer mode where the players can face against each other in a 1v1 fight. There are also various events with attacks that can come from various directions, and the player must rely on visual prompts and sound effects to dodge them.

# Game mechanics
The game features several mechanics that sets it apart. The arena in which players fight will have various pickups from different weapons to other objects that one can use, throw or defend with and will spawn randomly. On top of that there are several events that can happen during the duel, from meteors crashing from above, pits opening in the ground, pillars rising that can change the topography of the arena to vegetables that stun you being thrown by the bored spectators. During the fights the player camera is constantly locked on to the opponent. This serves as both a way to increase the difficulty of the game and to solve the issue of the arcade machine controls where with just one joystick and a few buttons it would be difficult to easily move the character and the camera.

# Milestones
First milestone is to have a working map and an animated player.
Second milestone is to implement the enemy and the systems necessary for fighting (healt, hit detection etc.)
Lastly is the implementation of events and other systems like audio system and menus.
